What is an Organic Compound?

An organic compound is a compound that contains at least one carbon atom.

  • Organic compounds are incredibly common in nature and they also make up things like DNA
  • Organic compounds can also be synthesized, making things such as plastic and rubber
  • There are over fifteen million known organic compounds

Organic compounds are vital for life.

  • Most medications are organic compounds
  • All known living things are organic compounds
  • Organic chemistry focuses on the various ways organic compounds tend to react with each other

Why Does Carbon Bond?

There are so many carbon-based compounds due to the way carbon shares electrons with its surrounding atoms.

  • Take a moment to review the periodic table and note that carbon is on the second row, with multiple elements to the left and right of it
  • The elements that surround the left side of carbon tend to lose electrons
  • The elements on the right side of carbon tend to take electrons
  • This pulling and pushing results in carbon being able to share electrons

Covalent Bonds

The forces that hold organic compounds together are referred to as covalent bonds.

  • When organic compounds interact with each other, certain covalent bonds will break, but other ones can form
  • Covalent bonds break when atoms stop sharing electrons
  • Covalent bonds form when atoms start sharing electrons
