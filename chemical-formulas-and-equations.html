Chemical Formulas

A substance's chemical formula is a line of text that represents the cehmical composition of the substance

  • It shows which elements are present and in what ratios
  • You can think of it as representing the number of atoms of each element present in a substance
  • A number called a subscript follows each elemet symbol and it demonstrates how many of thats specific element are in a molecule of that compound
  • For example, water's chemical formula H2O indicates that a molecule of water has two hydrogen atoms per single oxygen atom

Chemical formulas that have two or more of the same compound show it with a parenthesis

  • For example, (OH)2 means that there are 2 hydroxide (OH) molecules
  • When you come across something like this, all you have to do to figure out the amount of elements in a molecule of the compound is multiply the numbers inside the parenthesis by the number outside of it and the resulting numbers show the amount of elements in the compound

A structural formula is different than a chemical formula in that a structural formula also shows the in which the atoms are connected.

  • The lines that you will sometimes see connecting chemical symbols are meant to represent chemical bonds between the elements
  • These chemical bonds hold atoms together at various angles and distances
